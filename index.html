I am a Ph.D candidate in Philosophy at Johns Hopkins University. My primary research is in the philosophy of physics, with an emphasis on foundations of general relativity and thermodynamics/statistical mechanics. My dissertation concerns several foundational issues in black hole physics, including black hole thermodynamics and the black hole information loss problem.

I have secondary interests in philosophy of statistics. In particular, I am interestd in the role that simplicity plays in statistical inference, as well applications of information theory to statistical model selection.


During the 2020-2021 academic year I will be a Graduate Student Cross-Training Fellow, spending the year studying astrophysics in the physics and astronomy department at JHU. The fellowship is supported by the Templeton Foundation and administered by the Society for Christian Philosophers. I am very grateful for the opportunity.
