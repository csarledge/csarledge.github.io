Dissertation

My dissertation is a focused look at one of the most puzzling problems in theoretical physics: the black hole information loss paradox.  In the dissertation I clarify what the problem is and argue that there is a paradox to be dealt with, contrary to some recent claims.  I also consider various solutions to the problem, including black hole complementarity, holography, and quantum foundations.


Philosophy of Physics

My primary research trajectory in philosophy of physics is concerned with implications of spacetime theories. My recent effort has been directed towards the black hole information loss problem, as well as the resurgence of the infamous "hole argument". I am also investigating the role that quantum decoherence plays in the formation of large scale structure during inflation.

Philosophy of Statistics

My primary interests in philosophy of statistics center around appeals to simplicity and competing statistical paradigms. My master's thesis is concerned with the use of model selection as a justification for simplicity as an epistemic virtue. I am still actively thinking about these issues, especially as applied in cosmology and astrophysics (e.g. dark energy model selection).
