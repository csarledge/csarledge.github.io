Epistemology of Astrophysics

My primary research program centers on epistemological issues in astrophysics. Some topics I am currently working on include simplification in galactic modeling, analytical vs. numerical reasoning and underdetermination of modified gravity and dark matter/dark energy.

Foundations of Spacetime Theories

My primary research trajectory in the foundations of physics is concerned with implications of spacetime theories. My recent effort has been directed towards the black hole information loss problem, as well as the resurgence of the infamous "hole argument". I also am looking into the thermodynamics of spacetime and entropic gravity.

Philosophy of Statistics

My interests in philosophy of statistics center around appeals to simplicity and competing statistical paradigms. My master's thesis is concerned with the use of model selection as a justification for simplicity as an epistemic virtue.
